<h2>Section 1: Driving licences and categories of vehicles </h2>
<p>You must hold a current driving licence or a learner permit before driving any motor vehicle in a public
    place. You can drive only the type of vehicle for which you hold a licence or learner permit. And you must
    carry your driving licence or learner permit with you at all times when driving. </p>

<h4>Conditions attached to categories </h4>
<h5><span class="text-info">Motorcycles</span></h5>
<p>Holders of a driving licence for Category A are entitled to drive motorcycles with an engine output of any
    size.</p>
<p>Holders of a driving licence in category A2 are entitled to drive a motorcycle with an engine output of 35kW
    or less, and a power to weight ratio not exceeding 0.02kW per kg and not derived from a motorcycle of double
    its power.</p>

<h5><span class="text-info">Trailers</span></h5>
<h5>Cars and trailers</h5>
<p>If you hold a full category B licence, you may tow a trailer only if:
    the total maximum authorised mass (MAM) of the trailer is 750kg or less; or
    the combined MAM of the vehicle and trailer is no more than 3,500kg.

</p>
<p>When you use a car to tow a heavier trailer, you <span class="text-danger">must</span> hold a category BE
    licence. A driver with a car, small
    van or 4x4 and trailer licence category BE can draw a trailer where the MAM of the vehicle and trailer
    combination is greater than 3500kg but less than 7000kg.</p>
<h5>Heavier vehicles and trailers</h5>
<p>You <span class="text-danger">must</span> hold a Category CE, C1E, DE or D1E licence if you want to tow a
    heavier trailer. These are the licences that entitle you to drive the combinations of the towing vehicles
    and the trailer (see page 12). Equivalent licences are EC, EC1, ED or ED1.</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Heavy goods vehicles and buses</span></h5>
<p>You <span class="text-danger">must</span> obey the law on tachographs. For full information please see www.
    rsa.ie.</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">The Driver CPC and you</span></h5>
<h5>New Professional Driver</h5>
<p>Since 30 September 2014, in order to become a professional bus or truck driver, you must take the following
    five steps:</p>
<p><b>Step 1:</b> Pass a driver theory test and get a driver theory test certificate for category C or D or both
    as appropriate;</p>
<p><b>Step 2:</b> PGet a learner permit in the relevant category;</p>
<p><b>Step 3:</b> Pass a 2-hour case study theory test (note Step 2 must be completed before Step 3 if under 21
    when applying for a C or CE licence or under 24 when applying for a D or DE licence);</p>
<p><b>Step 4:</b> Pass a 90-minute practical driving test, including questions and demonstrations on how you
    would carry out a series of checks; and</p>
<p><b>Step 5:</b> Pass a 30-minute practical test.</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Learner permit</span></h5>
<p>A learner permit is issued to allow a person to learn to drive. Before you apply for your learner permit you
    <span class="text-danger">must</span> pass your driver theory test (see pages 17–18).</p>
<p>You <span class="text-danger">must</span> have a current learner permit to learn to drive and to take your
    driving test. Your permit must be for the same category of vehicle as the one you will use in your test.</p>
<p>The test is designed to check your knowledge of topics such as:</p>
<p>Rules of the Road<br />
    Risk perception <br />
    Eco-driving<br />
    Hazard awareness<br />
    Good driving behaviour<br />
</p>
<p><span class="text-danger">All categories of licences are subject to review. To make sure you comply with EU
        and Irish road safety policy, you are advised to check the websitewww.rsa.ie regularly.</span></p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Full driving licence</span></h5>
<p>You need a full driving licence for the category of vehicle that you intend to drive. You can drive only the
    category or categories of vehicle for which the licence is issued.</p>

<h5><span class="text-info">Applying for your full licence</span></h5>
<p>You <span class="text-danger">must</span> apply for your driving licence in person at any one of the
    National Driver Licence Service (NDLS) centres located around the country. When attending your
    appointment at the NDLS centre, you <span class="text-danger">must</span> bring with you:</p>
<p>a completed application form;
    the relevant fee;
    your current or most recent learner permit; and
    evidence of your PPSN and address (if changed) dated within last 6 months.
</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Novice drivers</span></h5>
<p>A driving licence is normally valid for 10 years for cars and motorcycles or 5 years for trucks and
    buses; you should renew your licence before this period passes. To renew your licence, you <span class="text-danger">must</span> apply in
    person to one of the NDLS centres . You will need to bring:</p>
<p>a completed application form and fee,
    your current or most recent full licence, and
    evidence of your PPSN and address (if changed) dated within last 6 months.
    If you are renewing a category C1, C1E, C, CE, D1, D1E, D or DE licence, you <span class="text-danger">must</span> include a medical report.
</p>
<p>You should apply to renew your licence not later than three months before it expires.</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Carrying a driving licence</span></h5>
<p>Remember, you <span class="text-danger">must</span> carry your driving licence, or learner permit, with
    you at all times when you are driving.</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Driving legally</span></h5>
<p>Before taking any vehicle on to the road you <span class="text-danger">must</span> be able to answer
    ‘yes’ to the following questions:</p>
<p>Are you carrying your driving licence or learner permit? Is the motor vehicle taxed?
    Is the tax disc on the windscreen?
    Is the insurance cover up to date and valid to cover you? Is the insurance disc on the windscreen?
</p>
<p>Is there an N-plate sign at the front and rear of your vehicle? (if you are a novice driver)</p>
<p>s the vehicle roadworthy and does it have an up-to-date National Car Test (NCT) Certificate on the
    windscreen? (if the vehicle is over four years old but not a taxi)</p>
<p>Does the vehicle have a Certificate of Roadworthiness? (if you are using a coach, bus, ambulance, goods
    vehicle or goods trailer and it is over a year old)</p>
<p>Are you carrying your CPC qualification card (as required) and digital tachograph card (as required)? (if
    you are a professional driver)</p>

<h5><span class="text-info">Tax</span></h5>
<p>All motor vehicles <span class="text-danger">must</span> be taxed before the vehicle is taken on the
    road.</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Insurance</span></h5>
<p>All drivers must have insurance covering them to drive a vehicle in a public place. By law you <span class="text-danger">must</span> inform the insurance company of relevant information</p>
<h5><span class="text-info">Vehicles that do not need to display an insurance disc</span></h5>
<p>Motorcycles (with or without a side car)
    Tractors
    Vehicles showing a trade licence
    Vehicles owned or used by an exempted person as defined by the Road Traffic Acts, for example members of
    emergency services
</p>
<p><b>All trailers <span class="text-danger">must</span> be covered by third party motor insurance. This
        applies whether the trailer is being towed or parked in a public place.</b></p>
<p><b>Even though the trailer does not have to display an insurance disc, you must have valid insurance
        cover.</b></p>
<h5><span class="text-info">National car test</span></h5>
<p>Vehicle testing makes sure your vehicle is safe to use on the road. This is especially important for
    older vehicles.</p>
<p>Passenger cars over 4 years old class="text-danger">must</span> have a valid NCT Certificate and show the
    NCT disc on the windscreen.</p>
<p>Passenger cars between 4 and 10 years old class="text-danger">must</span> be tested every 2 years.
    Passenger cars over 10 years old must be tested every year.</p>
<p>You may also avail of ‘voluntary early testing’ and get your car tested earlier than 90 days before its
    NCT due date.</p>
<p>Further information on the NCT is available on the National Car Testing Service website, www.ncts.ie.</p>
